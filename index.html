The feeling

You already know exactly what you need to do.
You understand the consequences of not doing it.
You might even know how.
And still. You cannot start.

There's a name for this. Aware paralysis. The gap between knowing and doing, with nothing to explain why it's there. The longer it goes, the worse it gets. And you can't explain it to people who haven't felt it. "Just do it" is what you get. "You were fine yesterday." And you stand there knowing they're right on every practical level and completely wrong on every real level, with no language for the gap.

GoMaiT doesn't ask you to explain yourself.
It just says: right, you're here now.
Let's see what we can do with the next ten minutes.

Adults with ADD and ADHD lose an estimated 22 to 27 working days a year to the symptoms. Not to laziness. Not to poor character. To a brain that works differently and has never had the right kind of help.

Questions

The things you're probably thinking right now

Honest answers. No marketing fluff.

I've tried apps before. They never work for me. Why would this be different?
Most apps assume you'll show up consistently, maintain a system, and not miss a day. That's the whole problem right there. GoMaiT was built by someone with ADD, for his family and for people like them. It works in the mess. It doesn't need you to get organised first. Miss a week. Buddy doesn't mention it. There's no system to fall off.
Is this just another productivity app I'll use for a week and forget?
Honestly, it depends on whether you use it. What we can tell you is that we've deliberately removed everything that makes apps fail for this kind of brain: streak counters, complicated setup, guilt when you go quiet. Come back after a month and Buddy picks up without a word about where you've been. The rest is up to you.
Is it only for people with ADHD or ADD?
No, and the distinction matters. ADD and ADHD are related but different, and GoMaiT isn't built exclusively for either. The founder has ADD, not ADHD. No formal diagnosis, but he's lived every symptom his whole life and has no doubt about it whatsoever. The feeling the app is built around, knowing what you need to do and not being able to start, shows up across ADD, ADHD, autism, anxiety, depression, and plenty of people who've never had any diagnosis at all. We don't ask for a label. If the feeling resonates, the app is for you.
What does "agentic" actually mean?
It means Buddy can actually do things, not just talk about them. Draft that email you've been avoiding. Build a document. Organise your calendar. Turn a voice note into a CV. You see everything before anything goes anywhere. Nothing sends without you saying so.
Is my data private? Especially my mental health data?
We don't sell it. We don't advertise with it. What you share with Buddy stays with Buddy. The mental health and crisis layer gets extra care. It's there to help you, full stop. Full privacy policy at launch. Short version: what happens in GoMaiT stays in GoMaiT.
What if I miss days? Does GoMaiT judge me for it?
No streak counters. No broken chains. Come back after two weeks and Buddy says hello like you never left, no nudges, no passive-aggressive "welcome back after 14 days". A missed day is just a day. What you do today is what matters.
When does it launch and what platforms?
iOS and Android, both at the same time. Waitlist members get in first and get the early bird pricing. Signing up is free and doesn't commit you to anything. It just puts you at the front of the queue.
What does the lifetime deal actually include?
50 agentic actions a month, every month, for life, at a single one-off payment. It covers the Agentic tier as it exists at launch, plus future feature additions to that tier. It doesn't include any new tiers created above Agentic, changes to the 50 actions cap, or premium add-ons introduced after purchase. No money changes hands until the app is ready and the deal formally opens. If it doesn't ship, you owe nothing.
